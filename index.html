Hydrothermal Mixed Waste Conversion System

A thermochemical platform designed to convert mixed municipal waste streams into fuels and chemical feedstocks using hydrothermal processing.

Project Overview

Municipal waste streams contain complex mixtures of organic materials, plastics, and textile residues that are difficult to process using traditional recycling systems. Most of these materials are disposed in landfills, leading to environmental pollution and energy loss.

The proposed system introduces a hydrothermal reactor that converts mixed waste directly into valuable outputs such as fuels and chemical feedstocks while minimizing the need for sorting or drying.

Project Objective

Convert mixed waste into energy products
Reduce landfill dependency
Utilize high moisture waste streams
Recover chemical and thermal energy

Waste Management Challenges

Mixed Waste Streams

Municipal waste contains food waste, plastics and textiles which are difficult to separate.

Moisture Content

Organic waste often contains high water content making thermal processing inefficient.

Plastic Contamination

Contaminated plastics are rejected by recycling systems.

Landfill Impact

Large waste volumes lead to methane emissions and environmental damage.

Hydrothermal Conversion Solution

Hydrothermal conversion uses high temperature and pressure water conditions to break down complex organic and polymeric materials. This allows simultaneous processing of plastics and organic waste.

Thermal Depolymerization

Plastic polymers break into smaller hydrocarbon molecules.

Organic Liquefaction

Organic materials convert into bio-oil and energy rich compounds.

Reactive Water Medium

Water acts as both solvent and reactant enhancing chemical conversion.
