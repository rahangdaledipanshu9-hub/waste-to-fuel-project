Mixed Waste Hydrothermal Conversion System

Turning Municipal Waste into Fuels, Chemicals and Energy

Project Overview

This project proposes a single-reactor hydrothermal conversion system designed to process mixed municipal waste. The reactor accepts food waste, textiles, and contaminated plastics without the need for sorting, washing, or drying. By eliminating these preparation steps, the system reduces energy consumption and lowers operational costs while maximizing material recovery.

Waste Feedstock

  • Food scraps and organic waste
  • Textile materials such as cotton and polyester
  • Mixed plastics including polyolefins and PET
  • Contaminated or wet municipal waste streams

Food waste provides hydrogen that improves the efficiency of hydrothermal liquefaction, enhancing the conversion of other materials in the waste mixture.

Conversion Technology

The process uses hydrothermal liquefaction with high temperature water and high pressure. Under these controlled conditions, complex waste materials break down into smaller molecules that can be recovered as fuels and chemical feedstocks.

  • Cellulose from textiles converts into sugars
  • Polyester breaks into oil intermediates and monomers
  • Polyolefins convert into liquid hydrocarbons
  • PET decomposes into reusable chemical feedstocks

Process Efficiency

Feedstock Conversion

80–95%

Liquid Product Yield

70–85%

Energy Recovery

65–75%

Integrated heat exchangers and gas recycling systems recover energy from the process, allowing the reactor to operate with minimal external energy input.

Reactor Engineering

  • Modular high-pressure reactor design
  • Corrosion-resistant construction materials
  • Smart chlorine management
  • Catalyst recovery system
  • Adaptive control system for variable waste composition

The reactor design allows stable operation even when waste composition changes daily.

Economic Benefits

By eliminating sorting and drying stages, the system reduces both capital and operational costs. Multiple valuable outputs are produced, including fuels, monomers, chemicals, and recoverable heat. These diversified revenue streams improve investment returns and shorten payback periods.

Applications

  • Municipal waste management facilities
  • Industrial waste processing plants
  • Decentralized waste-to-energy systems
  • Circular economy chemical production
