Mixed Waste Hydrothermal Conversion System

Transforming Municipal Waste into Fuel and Chemical Resources

Project Overview

This project introduces a single reactor hydrothermal system capable of processing mixed municipal waste including food scraps, textiles and contaminated plastics without the need for sorting, washing or drying. By directly processing wet and mixed waste streams, the system reduces preparation costs and energy consumption while maximizing material recovery.

Conversion Technology

The system uses hydrothermal liquefaction under high temperature and high pressure conditions. This environment allows complex waste materials to break down into useful fuels and chemical intermediates.

  • Cellulose from textiles converts into sugars
  • Polyester breaks into oil intermediates and monomers
  • Polyolefins convert into liquid hydrocarbons
  • PET converts into reusable chemical feedstocks

Process Efficiency

Feedstock Conversion

80–95%

Liquid Yield

70–85%

Energy Recovery

65–75%

Integrated heat exchangers and gas recycling systems help recover energy from the process, allowing the reactor to operate with minimal external energy input.
